Internet pioneers

Learn more about the pioneers of the internet below.

Vannevar Bush

(March 11,1890-June 28,1974)
American enginner,inventor and scientific administrator

He was responisble for developing the concept "memex" in 1930s ,a theoretical machine that allowed users to store and retrive information linked by associations. Bush influnced many inventions like the invetion of the mouse, hypertext and hypermedia.

More about Vannevar Bush

Doug Engelbart

(January 30, 1925 – July 2, 2013)
American enginner and inventor

He is famous for his contribution to the field of human-computer interaction, which he was developing. Engelbart is credited for a number of innovations, including the computer mouse, a multi-author hypermedia system, shared-screen teleconferencing,and computer-aided meetings.

More about Doug Engelbart

Ted Nelson

(Born June 17, 1937)
American pioneer of information technology, philosopher and sociologist

In 1960, he presented "Project Xanadu," a system for storing and connecting document fragments. He has spent much of his adult life working on Xanadu and promoting it. The terms hypertext and hypermedia were also established by Nelson.

More about Ted Nelson

Tim Berners-Lee

(Born 8 June 1955)
English computer scientist and inventor

He is best known as the inventor of the World Wide Web. His implementation between communication a Hypertext Transfer Protocol (HTTP) client and server via the Internet in mid-November 1989 was very successful.

More about Tim Berners-Lee

Steve Jobs

(February 24, 1955 – October 5, 2011)
American business magnate, industrial designer, investor, and media proprietor

He is widely recognized as a pioneer of the personal computer revolution of the 70s and 80s. Jobs was the chairman, chief executive officer (CEO), and co-founder of Apple Inc., the chairman and majority shareholder of Pixar, a member of The Walt Disney Company's board of directors following its acquisition of Pixar, and the founder, chairman, and CEO of NeXT.

More about Steve Jobs

Steve Wozniak

(Born August 11, 1950)
American electronics engineer, programmer, philanthropist, and technology entrepreneur

He is widely recognized as one of the prominent pioneers of the personal-computer revolution. Wozniak co-founded with his business partner Steve Jobs, the Apple Inc.

Marc Andreessen

(Born July 9, 1971)
American entrepreneur, investor, and software engineer

He is best known for his work at Mosaic, Netscape, Opsware, etc. During his career, Andreesen has also founded Andreessen Horowitz and invested in many successful companies including, Facebook, Foursquare, GitHub, Pinterest, LinkedIn and Twitter. More about Marc Andreessen

Eric J.Bina

(Born October 1964)
American software programmer

He is best known as the co–founder of Mosaic, and Netscape. With Marc Andreessen, Bina authored the first version of Mosaic while working as a programmer at National Center for Supercomputing Applications (NCSA) at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

More about Eric Bina

Robert Hartill

(Born 30 January 1969)
British computer programmer and web designer

He is best known for his work on the Internet Movie Database website, the Apache web server and is notable for playing a key role in the initial growth of the World Wide Web.

More about Rob Hartill

Louis Montulli

(Born in 1971)
American computer programmer

He is best known for his work in producing web browsers. Montulli co-authored a text web browser called Lynx, with Michael Grobe and Charles Rezac, while studying at the University of Kansas.

More about Lou Montulli

Vinton Gray Cerf

(Born June 23, 1943)
American Internet pioneer

He is best known as one of "the fathers of the Internet". Cerf contributed a lot working on TCP/IP and the Internet Society.

More about Vint Cerf

Bob Kahn

(Born December 23, 1938)
American electrical engineer

He is best known as one of "the fathers of the Internet". Kahn along with Vint Cerf, first proposed the Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) and the Internet Protocol (IP).
