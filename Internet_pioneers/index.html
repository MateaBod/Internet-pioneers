The Web ≠ The Internet

The majority of people are ignorant of the difference between the web and the internet.
The world wide web refers to the sites you access while you are online on a device,
while the internet refers to the network of linked computers that the web runs on,
as well as the transmission of emails and data.

Origins of the internet

Internet is a worldwide network of interconnected computers and related equipment.

Hundreds of pioneering scientists, engineers, and programmers built all of the features and technology that formed the Internet as we know it today. But its beginnings go back to 1969 were an idea of an “Intergalactic Network” of computers developed. Briefly followed, the idea of "packet switching," a way for efficiently transporting electronic data which became one of the internet's most important building elements. ARPANET, or the Advanced Research Projects Agency Network, was the first working prototype of the Internet.

Origins of the World Wide Web

The Web is a system of interlinked hypertext documents connected by the Internet

as another the internet-based notion is built in part on existing services and in part on a new protocol known as HTTP (HyperText Transfer Protocol). On the Internet, the WWW is a collection of HTTP servers. The Web is to blame for a big share of the current growth in Internet activity. Many companies and people are now building Web client and server software, and many more are utilizing these technologies for a wide range of applications. The Network uses hypertext technology to connect a web of documents, which may consist of text, pictures, sound, video, and other multimedia elements. With the launch of the Mosaic web browser in 1993, the web's popularity increased. Tim Berners-Lee created the Web in the 90s.

Email

Email, brief for "electronic mail," is one of the most generally utilized highlights of the Web. It permits you to send and get messages to and from anybody with an e-mail address, all over the world.


Telnet

This program is launched in 1969, it permits you to log in to a Unix computer through a text-based interface. If you have an account on a Telnet server, you could get admission to certain resources on the system which include your home directory, your email account, FTP files, etc. The disadvantage of Telnet to the majority is that, to apply it, you need to know Unix commands.


SSH

SHH is short for "Secure Shell." SSH is a way of securely communicating with some other computer.


FTP

FTP is short for "File Transfer Protocol." FTP is a protocol designed for transferring files over the Internet.


HTTP

HTTP is short for "Hypertext Transfer Protocol." HTTP is a protocol used to transfer data over the web.


Gopher

The gopher system lets in people to look for and retrieve data using a textual content interface.


browser

A browser is a system that allows you to access and see websites. Microsoft Internet Explorer, Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, and Apple Safari are  leading  browsers.


Lynx

For command-line interfaces, Lynx is one of the most popular text-based web browsers. It was founded in 1992 and is the oldest web browser currently in use.


Mosaic

Mosaic was one of the first web browsers. With the release of the Mosaic in 1993, the web's popularity increased. Mosaic's claim to fame was integrating text and graphics with multimedia.


hypertext

Hypertext is a text that provides links to other information. A user can easily move to other contents by clicking on a link in a hypertext document. The first widely accessible Hypertext was created by Apple in 1987, the authoring system HyperCard.
